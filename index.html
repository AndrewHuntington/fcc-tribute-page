Carl Sagan

The people's scientist

Below is a timeline of Carl Sagan's life:

  • 1934 - Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1939 - Not yet five, he attends New York's World Fair. He was so impressed by the exhibits that he was able to recall them years later
  • 1960 - Earns his Ph.D. in astrophysics. Begins his career as a Miller Fellow at the University of California, Barkley
  • 1971 - Co-designs the first physical message aimed at extraterrestrial intelligence known as the Pioneer plaques
  • 1973 - His book 'Jermone Agel, The Cosmic Connection: An Extraterrestrial Persepctive' is published and turns him into a popular science writer
  • 1978 - Wins the Pulitzer Prize for 'The Dragon of Eden'
  • 1980 - 'Cosmos', his best known work, is published. In the same year 'Cosmos: A Personal Voyage' debuts on television
  • 1985 - Publishes 'Contact'
  • 1994 - Publishes 'Pale Blue Dot: A Vision of the Human Future in Space'
  • 1995 - His last major work 'The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark' is published, in which he tries to explain the scientic method to layperson and encourage skeptical thinking
  • 1996 - Died, aged 62, in Seattle, Washington
